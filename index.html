The idea

A star in a bottle, on your table.

Fusion is how the Sun makes light — squeezing hydrogen so hard that nuclei merge and release energy. On Earth, scientists chase it inside machines that hold plasma at 100 million degrees with nothing but magnetic fields. Plasma Physics Trading Cards shrink those machines down to your palm.

Get to know fusion

Four ideas behind every reactor

The same concepts you can explore inside the app — here's the short version.

01

Plasma

A gas so hot its atoms break into free-moving charged particles — the fourth state of matter, and what stars are made of.

02

Magnetic bottle

No wall can touch 100-million-degree plasma. Magnetic field lines act like invisible rails that trap it in mid-air.

03

Many shapes

Tokamaks, spherical tokamaks, field-reversed configurations — each design is a different bet on how to hold plasma best.

04

What to notice

Ask: what shape is the field? How big is the machine? What question was it built to answer — power, control, or space travel?

Using it

How it works

  1. Print a plasma trading card, or grab one at an outreach event.
  2. Open Plasma Physics Trading Cards and point the camera at the card.
  3. The matching 3D reactor rises off the card, anchored in real space.
  4. Tap Save to My Library — then spin, zoom, and read it any time, no card needed.

Always fresh

Models stream from this site, so new reactors appear in the app the moment they're published — no app update required.

Built with

Unity + AR Foundation, running Apple ARKit and Google ARCore under the hood. The physical card is the controller — move it, and the reactor moves with it.
